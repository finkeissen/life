# Projects

Projects are **media projections** derived from the core architecture of `life`.

## Terminology

The **master glossary** lives at `foundation/core/glossary.md`.
Project documents should reference it rather than redefining terms locally.

 They do not define the core.
 They do not complete it.
 They do not resolve it.

Each project represents a **bounded cut**
through the architecture,
making certain aspects visible
while necessarily obscuring others.

Projects are therefore not additive.
They are **partial, selective, and loss-bearing** by design.

---

## Purpose of the project layer

The project layer exists to:

- test the architectural core under concrete instantiation
- explore how different media emphasize different structural aspects
- expose blind spots, omissions, and overrepresentations
- make visible which dimensions of life remain insufficiently articulated

Projects are not evaluated by aesthetic success,
but by **structural honesty**:
whether they remain consistent with the core
while accepting their own limitations.

---

## Required structure of a project

Each project folder must contain a `README.md`
that explicitly states:

- which **core aspects** are explored
- which aspects are **emphasized or exaggerated**
- which aspects are **deliberately left aside**
- which **form constraints** apply
- how **avatars**, if used, function structurally

Projects that do not perform this self-positioning
are considered **incomplete**.

## Current project domains (placeholders)

The following folders represent **domains of projection**.
They are **not exhaustive** and **not complete**.

Their presence does **not** imply adequate coverage.

### `novel/`

Long-form narrative projection.

Typical strengths:
- interiority
- temporal dilation
- reflective distance
- gradual development

Typical blind spots:
- bodily immediacy
- simultaneity
- non-verbal relation

Status:
- ⚠️ **structurally under-specified**
- no canonical novel project defined yet

---

### `opera/`

Musical-dramatic projection.

Typical strengths:
- embodiment through voice
- collective resonance
- irreversibility of performed time
- mythic condensation

Typical blind spots:
- analytical clarity
- internal justification
- fine-grained causality

Status:
- ⚠️ **high potential, not yet instantiated**
- no explicit mapping to core layers yet

---

### `film/`

Audiovisual temporal projection.

Typical strengths:
- simultaneity
- relational dynamics
- environmental context
- contingency

Typical blind spots:
- interior justification
- responsibility attribution
- structural abstraction

Status:
- ⛔ **not developed**
- represents a major gap in current projections

---

### `essay/`

Analytical and reflective projection.

Typical strengths:
- conceptual precision
- explicit structure
- traceable argumentation

Typical blind spots:
- lived immediacy
- affective resonance
- temporal pressure

Status:
- ⚠️ **partially present**
- risks overrepresenting clarity and coherence

---

### `installation/`

Spatial and experiential projection.

Typical strengths:
- embodiment
- spatial relation
- non-linear access
- silence and absence

Typical blind spots:
- narrative continuity
- responsibility tracing
- explicit development

Status:
- ⛔ **not developed**
- currently a conceptual placeholder only

## Cross-cutting absences (known gaps)

Independent of medium, the following aspects are
**not yet sufficiently projected**:

- structural interest conflicts
- mundane, non-dramatic adequacy
- quiet forms of maturity
- failure without catastrophe
- development without narrative arc
- sustained misalignment without resolution

These absences are **intentional placeholders**,
not oversights.

They mark **future pressure points** for new projects.

---

## Non-canonical status

No project is definitive.

Multiple projects may contradict each other
without contradiction of the core.

A project may be abandoned,
superseded,
or remain fragmentary
without loss to the architecture.

---

## Reminder

Projects are not answers.

They are **exposures**.

What remains unprojected
is as important
as what becomes visible.


---

## Cardinality and repetition

The architecture of `life` does **not** prescribe
a fixed number of works.

Nevertheless, different media exhibit
different **structural saturation points**.

Some projections benefit from repetition.
Others lose structural information when multiplied.

The following indications are **heuristic**, not prescriptive.

They describe **where additional works tend to add structure**
and where they tend to produce variation only.

---

### Novel

A novel functions as a **deep interior projection**.

It explores:
- temporality of self-understanding
- gradual development and unreife
- internal misalignment
- reflective distance

Structural note:
- Multiple novels tend to multiply perspective,
  not structure.

Indicative cardinality:
- **one novel is structurally sufficient**
- additional novels are optional variations

---

### Libretto

A libretto functions as a **compression layer**.

It reduces life to:
- voice
- decision
- scene
- irreversibility

Structural note:
- The libretto is not an end product.
- It is a reductive hinge between architecture and performance.

Indicative cardinality:
- **one libretto is sufficient**
- additional libretti add little structural novelty

---

### Opera

Opera functions as a **collective and embodied projection**.

It uniquely exposes:
- simultaneity of action
- conflict between voices
- irreversibility of performed time
- mythic condensation without synthesis

Structural note:
- Different operas can project different architectural layers.
- Repetition here can expose structure, not redundancy.

Indicative cardinality:
- **up to five operas are structurally meaningful**
- each corresponding to a distinct architectural cut

---

### Film

Film functions as a **synthetic and high-risk projection**.

It combines:
- simultaneity
- environment
- contingency
- narrative closure pressure

Structural note:
- Film tends to smooth misalignment.
- It risks producing false coherence.

Indicative cardinality:
- **zero or one film is sufficient**
- multiple films require strict structural differentiation

---

## Non-binding nature

These indications:
- do not constitute a plan
- do not define milestones
- do not imply completeness

They serve only to:
- prevent premature multiplication
- preserve architectural pressure
- keep projects responsive to necessity rather than ambition

No projection is required
unless a structural aspect remains unexposed.

## Project creation rule

A project must:

- declare its projection angle
- reference the core architecture
- accept structural limitation
- avoid redefining core terminology

Projects are not expansions of the core.
They are constrained projections.
