# Zone Model (A–E)

## Zone A – Supported Self-Evidence
Order appears natural.
Responsibility seems distributed.
Protection layers are intact.

## Zone B – Irritation
Contradictions surface.
Language begins to strain.
Confidence becomes effort.

## Zone C – Non-Delegability
Responsibility cannot be transferred.
Procedure fails as protection.
Signatures become irreversible.

## Zone D – Loss of Moral Relief
Good intentions accelerate collapse.
Being right no longer protects.
Everyone is defensible; no one is innocent.

## Zone E – Final Openness / No Excuse
No external authority remains.
No justification survives.
Silence replaces explanation.

Zones must be ontologically distinct,
not merely intensified variations.
