# Zone Differentiation Tests

Each zone must represent an ontologically distinct condition.
Not a tonal variation.

## Zone A – Supported Self-Evidence
Possible: Delegation, shared belief in structure.
Not yet visible: structural collapse as inevitability.
Protection intact.

## Zone B – Irritation
Possible: Doubt, contradiction, minor instability.
Still possible: belief in restoration.
Protection strained.

## Zone C – Non-Delegability
Possible: direct consequence attachment.
Not possible: procedural insulation.
Protection collapses at the individual level.

## Zone D – Loss of Moral Relief
Possible: simultaneous rightness of conflicting actors.
Not possible: moral simplification.
Protection collapses at interpretive level.

## Zone E – Final Openness
Possible: action without justification.
Not possible: appeal to higher authority.
Protection collapses completely.

If two zones allow identical structural possibilities,
they are not distinct enough.
