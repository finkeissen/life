# Architecture – Exposure Vectors (Target Profiles)

This document defines possible dominant exposure vectors for the novel.

An Exposure Vector determines:
- which dimension of reality becomes maximally visible
- which illusion is primarily dismantled
- which structural risks increase
- which invariants are under strongest pressure

It does NOT define:
- plot
- characters
- sequence
- scenes
- dramatic arc

It defines gravitational emphasis.


---

# I. ABYSS DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Structural collapse, irreversibility, erosion of order.

## Illusion Targeted
"The system is fundamentally stable."

## Primary Effect
Loss accumulates visibly and materially.

## Structural Risk
Monotony through continuous darkening.
Escalation mistaken for qualitative change.

## Invariant Stress
Risk of implicit moralization ("this is wrong").
Must avoid accusatory tone.


---

# II. LIGHTNESS DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Surface normality, routine, procedural smoothness.

## Illusion Targeted
"If everything appears stable, it is stable."

## Primary Effect
Collapse is revealed indirectly through triviality.

## Structural Risk
Under-dramatization.
Reader may misread tone as neutrality.

## Invariant Stress
Risk of unintended comfort.


---

# III. CLARITY DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Perception, analytical lucidity, structural foresight.

## Illusion Targeted
"Understanding leads to control."

## Primary Effect
Knowledge becomes isolating rather than empowering.

## Structural Risk
Abstraction.
Loss of embodied presence.

## Invariant Stress
Risk of intellectual superiority.


---

# IV. BODY DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Fatigue, aging, stress, illness, non-delegable physical limits.

## Illusion Targeted
"Agency is purely mental or institutional."

## Primary Effect
Irreversibility enters through physiology.

## Structural Risk
Naturalistic drift.
Reduction to individual suffering.

## Invariant Stress
Must avoid melodrama.


---

# V. SYSTEM DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Institutions, procedures, documentation, structural inertia.

## Illusion Targeted
"Procedure ensures justice or order."

## Primary Effect
System persists beyond intention.

## Structural Risk
Depersonalization.
Emotional distance.

## Invariant Stress
Risk of bureaucratic satire tone.


---

# VI. RELATIONSHIP DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Projection, loyalty conflicts, misrecognition, isolation.

## Illusion Targeted
"Mutual understanding stabilizes structure."

## Primary Effect
Intimacy accelerates structural fragility.

## Structural Risk
Psychologization.
Reduction to interpersonal drama.

## Invariant Stress
Must prevent moral sentimentalism.


---

# VII. RESPONSIBILITY DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Non-delegable burden without recognition.

## Illusion Targeted
"Responsibility distributes naturally across actors."

## Primary Effect
Carrying becomes structurally isolating.

## Structural Risk
Martyr narrative misinterpretation.

## Invariant Stress
Must avoid heroic framing.


---

# VIII. RECURRENCE DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Repetition without redemption.
Structural patterns returning across contexts.

## Illusion Targeted
"Insight prevents repetition."

## Primary Effect
Recognition fails to interrupt structure.

## Structural Risk
Fatalism.
Deterministic reading.

## Invariant Stress
Must avoid explicit thesis tone.


---

# IX. MORAL ACCELERATION DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Good intentions amplifying systemic instability.

## Illusion Targeted
"Moral correctness prevents collapse."

## Primary Effect
Ethical action increases structural fragility.

## Structural Risk
Didactic undertone.

## Invariant Stress
No explicit critique rhetoric.


---

# X. SILENCE DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Non-intervention, withheld speech, exhausted language.

## Illusion Targeted
"Speaking resolves structural tension."

## Primary Effect
Silence becomes structurally active.

## Structural Risk
Opacity.
Reader disengagement.

## Invariant Stress
Must remain embodied, not abstract.


---

# XI. ORDER WITHOUT POWER DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Internal coherence without enforcement capacity.

## Illusion Targeted
"Order naturally generates authority."

## Primary Effect
Stability becomes provocation.

## Structural Risk
Heroic misreading.

## Invariant Stress
No moral superiority tone.


---

# XII. LEAKAGE DOMINANCE

## Core Exposure
Slow erosion rather than visible collapse.

## Illusion Targeted
"Breakdown is always dramatic."

## Primary Effect
Protection disappears incrementally.

## Structural Risk
Perceived stagnation.

## Invariant Stress
Qualitative shifts must remain perceptible.


---

# Structural Use

Only one Exposure Vector should be dominant in a single novel.

Secondary vectors may operate,
but they must not compete for gravitational control.

If two vectors appear equally dominant,
the architectural focus is unclear.

Before proceeding to dramaturgy,
the dominant Exposure Vector must be declared.
