# Dimensions – Reincarnation

This document defines the structural dimensions
along which works inside the Reincarnation space operate.

These are not themes.
They are axes of instability.

Plot does not arise from events,
but from movement along these dimensions.

No dimension resolves into synthesis.
They remain in tension.

---

# I. Ontological Dimensions

## 1. Order ↔ Chaos

Order is not rule.
Order is carrying capacity.

Chaos is not exception.
It is the result of uncarried order.

---

## 2. Time ↔ Recurrence

Time does not erase.
It accumulates.

Unresolved structure returns in altered form.

---

## 3. Irreversibility

Every decision closes something.
Loss is not reversible.

There is no structural reset.

---

## 4. Finitude / Limit

Body.
Death.
Judgment.
Escalation.

Certain limits are not negotiable.

---

# II. Epistemic Dimensions

## 5. Knowledge ↔ Effect

Clarity does not guarantee impact.

Understanding may remain structurally ineffective.

---

## 6. Language ↔ Silence

Language orders reality.
Language also conceals.

Silence may protect.
Silence may accelerate collapse.

---

## 7. Local Plausibility ↔ Global Blindness

Actors behave plausibly within their local field.

The system destabilizes globally.

No villain is required.

---

# III. Anthropological Dimensions

## 8. Ego ↔ Responsibility

Self-protection stabilizes identity.
Responsibility destabilizes it.

---

## 9. Fear ↔ Honesty

Fear motivates action.
Honesty destabilizes comfort.

Honesty is not virtue.
It is exposure.

---

## 10. Intention ↔ Consequence

Good intentions do not ensure good outcomes.

Failure may emerge from plausibility.

---

# IV. Systemic Dimensions

## 11. Delegation ↔ Non-Delegability

Some responsibility can be transferred.
Some cannot.

Crisis begins where delegation fails.

---

## 12. Rule ↔ Reality

Compliance is not order.

Procedural correctness may conceal instability.

---

## 13. Morality ↔ Structural Responsibility

Morality relieves.
Responsibility carries.

Moral framing may protect actors
while destabilizing systems.

---

## 14. Power ↔ Clarity

Power without order destabilizes.
Order without power does not survive.

This tension cannot be dissolved.

---

# V. Dramaturgical Dimensions

## 15. Height ↔ Collapse

Climax does not equal victory.

Visibility may accelerate breakdown.

---

## 16. Turning Point ↔ Further Loss

A turning point removes protection.
It does not provide solution.

---

## 17. Exposure ↔ Redemption

The work exposes.
It does not redeem.

No catharsis is required.

---

## 18. Exhaustion

The audience is not comforted.

Structural recognition replaces emotional relief.

---

# VI. Protagonist-Specific Dimensions

## 19. Order without Power

The protagonist carries order internally
without enforcing it externally.

Existence becomes provocation.

---

## 20. Desire to Act ↔ Refusal to Enforce

The wish to intervene remains legitimate.

Enforcement is refused.

The conflict is internal, not heroic.

---

## 21. Witness ↔ Intervention

The observer sees.

The observer does not act.

Responsibility is not removed by clarity.

---

# VII. Meta-Dimension

## 22. Recurrence without Redemption

Reincarnation is structural return.

Not salvation.
Not progress.
Not healing.

What is not carried
returns.

---

# Usage

When constructing scenes, ask:

- Which dimension shifts?
- Which protection is removed?
- Which tension becomes visible?
- Which loss becomes irreversible?

Do not ask:
"What happens next?"

Ask:
"What collapses next?"
