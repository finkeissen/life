# Architecture of Life

This document sketches the **structural architecture**
from which all media projects in this repository can be derived.

It is intentionally:
- minimal
- non-prescriptive
- non-narrative

The architecture describes **dimensions**, not stories.

---

## Layers (five)

### 1. Body / Matter

Finitude, vulnerability, metabolism, decay.

The body is not a symbol.
It is the primary constraint under which all action occurs.

Irreversibility first appears here.

---

### 2. Action / Motion

Decision, movement, intervention.

Action always:
- selects
- excludes
- commits

Every action introduces a **point of no return**,
even when motivated by coherence or good faith.

---

### 3. Relation / Mirror

Encounter with others.

Here emerge:
- conflict
- dependency
- recognition
- misrecognition

Relations generate feedback loops
that no individual actor fully controls.

---

### 4. Meaning / Myth

Interpretation, narrative, justification.

Meaning does not describe reality;
it organizes experience.

Myth stabilizes action
but increases the risk of global blindness.

---

### 5. Dissolution / Silence

Loss, breakdown, death, forgetting.

This layer marks the limit of articulation.
Not everything resolves into meaning.

---

## Relational principle

Life unfolds through **relations between layers**,
not within isolated layers.

Misalignment between layers
(e.g. action outrunning perception,
meaning masking bodily limits)
is a normal condition.

---

## Boundary principle

Every projection requires a cut:
a selection from an overrich world.

No work is complete.
Each carries loss.
