# Principles

This document lists **structural invariants**.
They do not explain life.
They constrain how life can be approached.

---

## Projection, not totality

No artifact in this repository represents life as a whole.

Every work is a projection:
- bounded
- selective
- loss-bearing

Completeness is structurally impossible.

---

## Constraint and irreversibility

Human action always occurs under constraint.

Some constraints are:
- temporal
- bodily
- social
- symbolic

Irreversibility is unavoidable.
Every decision closes alternatives.

---

## Misalignment

Perfect alignment between:
- perception
- self-understanding
- reality
is structurally impossible.

Degrees of misalignment are normal.

From misalignment may emerge:
- learning
- adaptation
- stagnation
- conflict
- failure

No single outcome is guaranteed.

---

## Responsibility

Boundaries create responsibility.

Responsibility arises:
- without full knowledge
- without guaranteed success
- without transferability

Responsibility cannot be delegated to:
- systems
- theories
- collectives
- audiences

---

## Development

Development is not progress.
It is not optimization.

It describes changes in **relative adequacy**
between capacity and constraint.

Development has no final state.

---

## Stability vs. movement

- The core is stable.
- Projects may evolve.

The core constrains projects.
Projects must not redefine the core.
