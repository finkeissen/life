# Core

The **core** holds the stable architecture of life.

It defines:
- structural dimensions (layers)
- irreducible boundaries and simplifications
- invariants that persist across media, tone, and interpretation

The core does **not** provide narratives, characters, or solutions.
It is not a theory of progress, meaning, or redemption.

The core specifies **what cannot be avoided**
when engaging with human life under conditions of finitude.

---

## Function

The core exists to:
- stabilize the repository against drift
- prevent implicit moralization or hidden teleology
- provide a shared structural reference for all projections

It answers **structural questions only**, such as:
- What kinds of limits are unavoidable?
- What relations recur across contexts?
- Where does responsibility inevitably arise?

## Change policy

The core should change **rarely**.

Permissible reasons for change:
- clarification of structure
- resolution of contradiction
- integration of a genuinely missing invariant

Impermissible reasons:
- adapting to a specific project
- aesthetic preference
- explanatory expansion

---

## Scope and abstraction level

The core operates at a **structural level of abstraction**.

It does not describe:
- individual biographies
- historical contingencies
- psychological profiles
- cultural specifics

These belong to projections.

The core remains intentionally
under-determined with respect to content,
while being over-determined with respect to constraints.

---

## Relation to projects

Projects **instantiate** the core.
They do not verify it,
complete it,
or supersede it.

A project may:
- stress-test a core aspect
- reveal a blind spot
- expose a tension
- fail to carry the structure

Such outcomes do **not** retroactively modify the core.

Only repeated, structurally consistent pressure
may justify core revision.

---

## Misalignment as organizing concept

The core assumes persistent **misalignment**
between:
- perception
- self-understanding
- reality

This misalignment is:
- structural
- non-pathological
- irreducible

Outcomes emerging from misalignment
may include learning, adaptation, conflict, stagnation, or failure.

No outcome is privileged as final or normative.

---

## Development without teleology

The core acknowledges **development**
as change in relative adequacy
between capacity and constraint.

Development:
- has no predefined direction
- has no final state
- does not imply improvement

Regression, stagnation, and repetition
are structurally admissible trajectories.

---

## Responsibility under constraint

Responsibility arises
where bounded agents act under conditions
they do not fully control or comprehend.

Responsibility:
- precedes understanding
- survives failure
- cannot be discharged by explanation or resonance

The core records this as a condition,
not as a moral injunction.

---

## Use as architectural reference

The core is intended to be:

- cited by projects
- referenced in project READMEs
- stable across time
- readable without knowledge of specific works

It is not intended to be:
- persuasive
- motivating
- exhaustive

---

## Incompleteness

The core is complete **as architecture**,
not as representation.

What it leaves open
is not a gap to be filled,
but a space in which projection becomes possible.

Silence at the core level
is a structural feature.
